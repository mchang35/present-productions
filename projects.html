PROJECTS:


Our current project is our first of many to come. "CONVENIENCE" is a short film that tells the story of a teen boy and his mother living in the underbelly of San Marcos, California struggling to feed themselves and pay their monthly rent. The teen boy, desperate to find a solution from becoming homeless, takes matters into his own hands by attempting to rob a local convenience store.

This non-profit project is a self-funded and community donated short film. The film in its final form will be publicly distributed on a multitude of social media platforms, and may be submitted to approved local film festivals. All locations for filming will remain anonymous. All production members (including actors and extras) will be donating their time and talent as unpaid work. However, everyone involved will be credited in the end credits, obtain portfolio building experience, along with gain potential social media exposure and recognition. Shooting will take place over the course of 4-5 nonconsecutive days in the middle of June, not including reshoots or extended days for filming. There could be work days that could include weekdays and/or weekends as needed. Scenes will be filmed at various locations throughout the city of San Marcos. We are excited to bring this short film to life and look forward casting, filming and producing this. If anyone wants to take part in any aspects of this production, please contact us.
